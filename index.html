About Me

I'm a computer engineer with a strong interest in microsensors and multisensor embedded platforms, constantly looking for new ways to integrate them into bigger networks.

I am now pursuing a Ph.D. in Engineering and Applied Science at the University of Bergamo, with a focus on designing, testing, and characterizing front-end circuits for cutting-edge High Energy Physics pixel detectors.

My background experience has given me knowledge of advanced programming, computer design, mechatronics, and microelectronics. In addition to my studies, I've obtained great experience in agile Scrum project management, effective code management, and the development of good team collaboration. These abilities were further developed while working as a web developer while completing university.

Current Work

I am currently a Research Fellow at the National Institute for Nuclear Physics (INFN), section of Pavia, working on the Falaphel project. In this role, my responsibilities include the design, test, and characterization of front-end circuits for the next generation of High Energy Physics pixel detectors.
